01 — The Name & Why It's Different
Sister brand · SkinDecide family
SkinDecide Baby
Safety-first · Minimalism by default · Not a product recommender
Baby /ˈbeɪbi/
Noun — the audience
Middle English · babe
A human in the first year of life. Skin that is 20–30% thinner than adult skin, with dramatically higher permeability, a still-developing acid mantle, and a surface-area-to-volume ratio that causes significantly greater systemic absorption of topical ingredients.
"Baby" is added as a suffix — not rebranded. This signals clear family membership while naming the specific, distinct audience. The science changes. The scrutiny doubles. The name reflects both.
Safety-first /ˈseɪfti fɜːst/
Compound adjective — the philosophy
Product positioning — not an ingredient
A fundamentally different market from adult skincare. Where SkinDecide optimises for efficacy, SkinDecide Baby optimises for risk minimisation. The app's purpose is not to recommend more products — it is to help parents use fewer.
This is the most important brand distinction. Most baby product brands sell more. SkinDecide Baby is the only brand that actively argues: "You probably don't need this." That counter-intuitive honesty is the brand's core differentiator.
Why This Is a Different Category
The problem
Parental anxiety + "hypoallergenic" and "mild" having no legal definition + product overload for something that requires minimal intervention. "100% natural" is not a safety guarantee — it is a marketing claim.
The biology
Baby skin absorbs ingredients more readily into the bloodstream. Fragrance, phenoxyethanol, essential oils, and penetration enhancers carry higher systemic risk. The scoring system applies adult-plus-50% caution thresholds.
The promise
Not a product comparison tool. A safety and minimalism assistant. The app actively tells parents what they do NOT need. Water and a mild cleanser is sufficient for most babies under 12 months.
02 — Color System & Meanings
Color philosophy: SkinDecide Baby's palette is softer and more muted than SkinDecide's parchment-and-ember. The sages, soft whites, and warm greens evoke calm, botanical gentleness — not clinical sterility. Every color choice signals: this is for something fragile and precious.
Dark Sage
#1C211A · Deep warm-green near-black
Ink that is green-toned, not black or warm brown. Distinct from SkinDecide's Ink (#1A1612) — the green undertone signals botanical safety, not editorial authority. All scientific text is set in Dark Sage.
Primary text · scientific authority · calm weight
Sage
#5A8F6A · Medium sage green
The brand accent — not a fire red or a warning amber, but a living green. Named after Salvia officinalis: a plant associated with healing, wisdom, and protection. Calmer than SkinDecide's Ember. The brand dot is Sage.
Brand accent · brand dot · verified · safe
Linen
#F0F4EE · Soft sage-tinged white
Warmer and greener than SkinDecide's Parchment. The colour of organic cotton muslin — the material of baby blankets. Softer, more yielding than the parent brand. The background is gentler because the subject is.
Background · softness · organic warmth
Evidence
#3D7A52 · Deep forest green
Used exclusively when something is evidence-backed and safe for baby use. A deeper, more serious green than the Sage accent — evidence claims deserve weight. Named for its function: this is green for "this has science behind it."
Evidence-backed · clinical · verified for babies
Caution
#8A7340 · Muted amber-ochre
The same functional role as SkinDecide's Amber — but softer and less saturated. In baby context, caution is never alarming — it is informative. "Be aware" not "be afraid." A parent sees amber and pauses. They do not panic.
Caution · awareness · conditional claims
Mossy
#5A6557 · Warm grey-green
Supporting text, labels, metadata. Green-toned grey — warmer than neutral grey, botanical without being botanical. Every piece of context information: dates, help text, secondary notes.
Secondary text · support · context · calm
03 — Brand Elements & Meanings
●
The Sage Dot
The same structural element as SkinDecide and HairDecide — but in Sage green. Family mark. At 10px (slightly larger than parent brand) — because the baby context requires more visibility and gentleness.
Family unity · gentleness · botanical safety
Baby
The Sub-Badge
The word "BABY" appears as a monospace sub-label beneath "SkinDecide" in all contexts. Never abbreviated. Never stylised. Always in Sage monospace — marking the category distinction clearly and calmly.
Category clarity · sub-brand position · calm labelling
↓
The Downward Arrow
Used in the Minimalism Indicator — pointing downward to signal: less is better. Unique to SkinDecide Baby. No other brand in the SkinDecide family uses this symbol. It is the anti-consumption icon.
Less · minimalism · safety through simplicity
✗
The "Not Needed" Mark
SkinDecide Baby is the only brand in the family that regularly tells users a product is NOT needed. The ✗ in this context does not mean danger — it means "this adds unnecessary risk without adding benefit." A different kind of honesty.
Subtraction · minimalism · risk reduction
pH 5.0–5.5
The pH Range
Baby skin's acid mantle develops over the first weeks of life. The optimal pH for baby skincare products is 5.0–5.5 — more acidic than many "gentle" products. This number appears in product guidance to ground abstract claims in measurable science.
Measurable truth · acid mantle science · infant skin biology
⬡
The Hexagon
Inherited from the SkinDecide parent brand — the molecular structure symbol. In baby context used rarely and only for ingredient analysis — it signals "this is science" without visual alarm. Shown outlined and muted.
Molecular analysis · inherited science mark · family link
04 — Typography & Meaning
Gentle by design.
Not by marketing.
Fraunces — Display
Same typeface as SkinDecide — family consistency. But: the scale is smaller (40px not 40px), the leading is tighter. Baby content does not need to shout. The sage italic replaces ember red — warmth without urgency. Fraunces's optical "wonkiness" is intentional: it is warm and imperfect, like the subject matter.

DM Mono — Body, Data & Claims
All ingredient names, regulatory references, pH values, risk levels. In SkinDecide Baby, DM Mono is used for Dutch-language claims analysis text — it feels like an official document, which is appropriate for regulatory content. The monospace signals: this is verifiable.
05 — Tagline System
Primary
Gentle by design.
Not by marketing.
"Gentle by design" claims intentional minimalism — product formulation and routine design choices that reduce risk. "Not by marketing" is the challenge: most baby products claim gentleness without defining it. This tagline separates the brand from that category entirely.
Mission
Niet: productvergelijker.
Wel: veiligheids-assistent
voor ouders.
Dutch-language mission statement. Immediately distinguishes by saying what the brand is NOT. Most apps are product comparers — SkinDecide Baby is not. The word "veiligheids-assistent" (safety assistant) positions it as a tool for parental peace of mind, not a shopping guide.
Minimalism
Water en een milde cleanser is genoeg.
The most radical statement in the brand system. Actively counter-marketing. Used in the Minimalism Indicator and the About page. It tells the truth that the baby product industry has no incentive to tell: for most healthy babies under 12 months, plain water and one gentle wash product is genuinely sufficient.
