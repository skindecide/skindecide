Brand Identity & Meaning System · 2025
01 — The Name: What It Means
SkinDecide
Verb-first · Action-oriented · No passive consumption
Skin /skɪn/
Noun — the subject
Old English · scinn
The largest organ of the human body. The interface between self and world. Not a canvas to be improved — a functional biological system to be understood.
By naming the product's subject first, the brand positions skin as something to be known scientifically — not beautified, transformed, or fixed. Skin is the starting point, not the problem.
Decide /dɪˈsaɪd/
Verb — the action
Latin · decidere — "to cut off"
From Latin de- (off) + caedere (to cut). Literally: to cut away uncertainty. To resolve doubt by eliminating alternatives. An active, rational choice.
The verb "decide" signals agency. Users are not passive receivers of product recommendations — they make informed choices. The Latin root "cut off" mirrors what the brand does: cuts through marketing noise to reach clarity.
Why the combination works
Structure
Noun + Verb. The most direct grammatical structure possible. No adjectives, no modifiers. This mirrors the brand philosophy: no excess.
Tension
"Skin" is biological and passive. "Decide" is cognitive and active. The brand sits exactly at that intersection — science meets personal agency.
Differentiation
Every beauty brand name suggests transformation or aspiration (Glow, Radiance, Pure). SkinDecide suggests thinking. A fundamentally different category signal.
02 — Brand Elements & Their Meanings
●
The Red Dot
A single point of precision. In scientific notation, a dot marks the decimal — the exact value. In optics, a single focused point of light. Always placed at top-right: the alert position.
Meaning: precision · focus · the moment of decision
Sk
The Initials Icon
Not an abstract symbol — actual letters. Chosen because "Sk" looks like the beginning of a scientific abbreviation (like "Sp." for species in taxonomy). Legible at any size.
Meaning: identity · taxonomy · belonging to a system
em
Italic Emphasis
In scientific writing, italics mark species names (Homo sapiens) and foreign terms — things requiring precise definition. SkinDecide uses italics for the key word in every headline.
Meaning: the word that carries the truth of the sentence
⬡
The Hexagon
The hexagon appears in chemistry (benzene ring), in biology (honeycomb, cellular structures), and in materials science. It's the most efficient packing structure in nature.
Meaning: molecular structure · natural efficiency · science
pH 4.5
Monospace Data
All data, percentages, and ingredient names are set in DM Mono — the typeface of lab reports, code, and clinical documentation. Data should look like data.
Meaning: objectivity · measurement · clinical honesty
—
The Em Dash
Used consistently where most brands would use a colon or period. The em dash signals a pause before precision — a beat before the exact fact arrives. Borrowed from scientific notation.
Meaning: pause · then precision · the beat before truth
03 — Color System & Meanings
Ink
#1A1612 · Near-black warm
The colour of printed text in scientific journals. Not pure black — warm-toned to suggest analogue, human knowledge rather than digital sterility. Information you can trust.
Function: primary text, authority, weight
Ember
#C8432A · Warm red-orange
Not the red of alarm or danger — the red of a heated element in a lab. The colour of azelaic acid's molecular model. Warm enough to feel human, saturated enough to demand attention.
Function: decision point, accent, brand mark
Parchment
#F5F0E8 · Warm off-white
The colour of aged paper — peer-reviewed journals, annotated research, prescription pads. Signals that this information has history and weight. Deliberately anti-digital, anti-glossy.
Function: background, breathing room, trust
Evidence
#2A7A4B · Forest green
Named after its function. The colour used exclusively when something is scientifically supported. In traffic systems, green means proceed safely. In SkinDecide, it means: this is real.
Function: verified, safe, proceed, science-backed
Caution
#B8780A · Amber-ochre
Amber: the colour used in laboratory warning labels for "handle with care". Not stop — but pay attention. Used for emerging evidence, vague claims, and ingredients that need context.
Function: nuance, proceed with awareness, vague
Stone
#6B6358 · Warm grey
The colour of concrete lab surfaces, the grey of neutral steel instruments. Context and supporting information — present and readable, but not competing for attention with primary content.
Function: secondary text, metadata, de-emphasis
04 — Typography & Its Meaning
Science,

Fraunces — Display
Named after Fraunces Tavern in New York, one of America's oldest. Designed by Phaedra Charles & Flavia Zimbardi — a "wonky" serif with optical size variation. At small sizes it's elegant; at large sizes its quirks emerge. Like scientific findings: the closer you look, the more interesting it gets. The italic is warm, humanist — emotion within rigour.

DM Mono — Body & Data
Monospace typefaces were designed for typewriters and terminals — where every character needed equal space for alignment. In SkinDecide, it signals that all data is treated equally and precisely. Percentages, ingredient names, and scores all look like they belong in a lab report. Designed by Colophon Foundry — a type studio known for functional, no-nonsense design.
05 — Tagline System & Meanings
Primary tagline
Less hype.
More science.
Fewer products.
Three sentences. Each one shorter in character count than the previous — visually tightening like a scientific conclusion. "Less" and "Fewer" are reductive — a rare move in an industry built on addition. "More science" is the only additive phrase, making it the emotional and logical centre. The full-stop after each clause forces a pause — read it slowly.
Decision tagline
Evidence-based beauty.
"Evidence-based" is borrowed from medicine — where it means treatment decisions made from published, peer-reviewed clinical evidence rather than tradition or anecdote. By applying it to beauty, SkinDecide makes a category-level claim: this is what medical-grade thinking looks like for skincare. The italic sets it apart — a definition, not a slogan.
Mission
Not an inspiration app.
A decision tool.
Deliberately negative positioning: by saying what it's NOT, it creates distance from the inspiration economy (Pinterest, TikTok, Instagram beauty). "A decision tool" is the direct functional claim — no metaphor, no aspiration. This mirrors the product itself: plain-language descriptions of what works and why.
