Hair /h…õr/

The keratinized filament growing from follicles in the dermis. Hair is dead tissue from the shaft upward ‚Äî only the follicle is alive. This distinction matters: products act on dead cells, not living biology.
HairDecide names its subject clinically ‚Äî not aspirationally. "Locks", "mane", "strands" all romanticize. "Hair" is biological and precise. It sets the tone for everything that follows.
Decide /d…™Ààsa…™d/
Verb ‚Äî the action
Latin ¬∑ decidere ‚Äî "to cut off"
From Latin de- (off) + caedere (to cut). Literally: to cut away uncertainty. In hair science context: to cut through marketing claims and cut off overconsumption. The verb is active, rational, definitive.
The same root as SkinDecide ‚Äî deliberate brand family consistency. "Decide" positions users as rational agents making informed choices, not passive beauty consumers.
Positioning Statement
The shift
From "beautiful hair" ‚Üí to "healthy scalp". The scalp is the foundation ‚Äî it is skin, with follicles, sebaceous glands, and a microbiome. Every hair problem starts there.
The problem
"Repair", "bond building", "keratin restoration" ‚Äî these terms are used without definition. Cosmetic effect vs. structural interaction are conflated. Overconsumption follows.
The promise
The dermatologist for your scalp in your pocket. Minimum effective routine. Maximum ingredient transparency. Marketing vs. molecule ‚Äî decoded.
